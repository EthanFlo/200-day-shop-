# Daily & Weekly Actions

What you personally do. Everything else I handle.

## Launch week (days 1–7)

**Day 1 — Tuesday or Wednesday, morning (45 min)**
- Upload `products/pack-01-claude-code-prompts/` to Gumroad
  - Product type: Digital product
  - File: zip the folder or upload `PROMPTS.md` + `README.md` + `LICENSE.txt` as separate files
  - Paste listing copy from `marketing/pack-01-gumroad-listing.md`
  - Create discount code `LAUNCH17` valid for 72 hours
- Post X thread from `marketing/pack-01-launch-content.md`. Schedule tweets 2 min apart via Typefully.
- Post Reddit. Watch for moderator response — some subs remove promo posts within minutes.
- Post LinkedIn (link in first comment, not the post body).

**Day 2 (10 min)**
- Reply to every comment on every post. Even one-liners. Algorithm rewards conversation.
- Post first Threads update.

**Day 3 (10 min)**
- Reply to remaining comments.
- Post second Threads update.

**Day 4–5 (5 min each)**
- Post drip content (I'll generate `marketing/drip/pack-01/week-1.md` for you to copy from).

**Day 6 (10 min)**
- End discount code.
- Post X/Threads "last chance" message (don't spam other platforms with this — looks thirsty).

**Day 7 (20 min) — Retro**
- Open Gumroad analytics. Check: sales count, UTM breakdown, refund rate, view-to-buy conversion.
- If conversion >3%: your listing works. Pour more traffic in.
- If conversion <1%: listing needs work. Ask me to rewrite.
- If sales are 0: don't panic. Week 1 with no audience often converts to 0–3.

## Steady state (after week 1, ~15 min/day)

**Monday (15 min)**
- Run `/loop` to invoke me for the weekly batch: new drip posts, performance review, next product draft.
- Review what I generated. Approve or adjust.

**Tuesday–Friday (5–10 min/day)**
- Copy one scheduled post into the platform that's scheduled for that day.
- Reply to any comments or DMs from the prior day.
- Check Gumroad inbox for refund requests or questions.

**Saturday (10 min)**
- Review week's numbers in Gumroad.
- If a product is outperforming: I'll draft a follow-up in the same niche.
- If a product is flat for 3 weeks: archive it, focus attention elsewhere.

**Sunday (0 min — off)**
- Seriously. Burnout kills shops faster than bad products.

---

## What to do when sales spike (nice problem)

- Do not raise prices mid-spike. Raise AFTER it settles so you learn the new baseline.
- Ask buyers (via Gumroad post-purchase email) what they'd want next. Their answer is your next product brief.
- If buyer questions come in: answer fast. Fast replies become screenshots that sell the next one.

## What to do when sales stall

- Do NOT throw 5 new products at the wall. Diagnose first.
- Ask me: "Run a diagnosis on pack-0X. What's the likely reason sales stalled?" I'll check listing copy, pricing, traffic mix, refund rate, and recommend one change.
- Make one change. Wait 2 weeks. Measure.

## The rule

One change at a time. Always measurable. Never panic-pivot.
