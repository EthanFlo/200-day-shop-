# Customer Response Templates

Pre-approved replies for common Gumroad buyer situations. Copy, fill placeholders, send. Keep it human — don't paste these word-for-word every time; vary a detail.

All replies go from your Gumroad inbox or the email address you configured for receipts.

---

## 1. "I bought the pack, great work — any chance of a discount on the next one?"

> Thanks — glad it's useful. Existing buyers get 40% off future packs. I'll email the code when the next one ships (expected {date}). If it helps you, a short public note on X/LinkedIn goes a long way too. No pressure either way.

---

## 2. "Do you have a team license?"

> Yes. Team pricing is $99 for up to 10 seats, $249 for up to 50. Single bill, single license doc. Want me to send a Gumroad invoice? What's the seat count and best email?

---

## 3. "Prompt #X doesn't work for my codebase — it's {language/framework}"

> That's useful feedback, thanks. Which language/stack, and what did Claude do wrong — wrong output, refused, or just generic? I patch the pack based on reports like this; you'll get the updated version auto-pushed via Gumroad.

---

## 4. Refund request

> Absolutely — refund processed. Takes 3–5 days to land back on your card. Mind telling me what didn't work? Not to talk you out of it, just helps me improve. No need to justify.

(Always process refund first, ask questions second. Within Gumroad's 30-day window it's automatic.)

---

## 5. "Can I share this pack with my coworker?"

> Licensing is per-user — your coworker should grab their own copy via {gumroad URL}. If there are 5+ of you, team license is cheaper and I can set it up directly. Sending the link helps keep the lights on so I can keep shipping packs.

---

## 6. "Is there a subscription or newsletter?"

> No subscription — one-time purchase, updates free for life via your Gumroad library. I do post new pack announcements on X @{handle}, which is the fastest way to hear about launches.

---

## 7. "How do you know these prompts work?"

> Every prompt in the pack came from my own daily use or from shipping fixes based on buyer reports. I don't ship prompts I haven't tested. If one doesn't work for your stack, tell me and I'll refine it — updates are free.

---

## 8. Generic "love it" / positive comment with no question

> Appreciate it — really does mean something when someone takes a minute to say it landed. If you get a chance, a brief post on X/LinkedIn tagging @{handle} helps the next person find the pack. Either way, glad it's useful.

---

## 9. Angry/complaint with no refund request

> You're right that {specific thing they criticized} isn't good enough. I'll fix it in the next update (~1 week) and push it to your Gumroad library. If you'd rather just refund, say the word and I'll process it — no hard feelings.

(Never argue. Own it. Either refund or fix.)

---

## 10. Press / podcast / partnership inquiry

> Interested — tell me more. What's the angle, audience size, timing? I can also share rough numbers on the shop if useful for the piece.

(Only engage if audience fit is real. Don't chase small podcasts that eat 4 hours for 3 listeners.)

---

## Rules

- **Speed matters more than polish.** Reply within 24h. Gumroad buyers screenshot fast replies and that drives next sales.
- **Never lie about numbers or testimonials.** One embarrassing public receipt kills the shop.
- **Refund first, investigate second.** Fighting refunds loses you more than it saves.
- **Every complaint is free product research.** Log recurring issues in `ops/status.md` under "Open experiments."
